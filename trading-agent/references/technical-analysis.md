# Technical Analysis Reference

## Trend Indicators

### Moving Averages
- **20 MA** — short-term trend. Price above = bullish momentum, below = bearish
- **50 MA** — medium-term trend. Institutional traders watch this closely
- **200 MA** — long-term trend. Golden Cross (50 crosses above 200) = major bullish signal. Death Cross = major bearish signal
- **EMA vs SMA** — EMA reacts faster to recent price, better for short-term. SMA smoother, better for long-term trend

**Key setups:**
- Price bounces off 50MA with volume = buy signal
- Price breaks below 200MA with volume = sell signal
- All MAs stacked bullishly (200 < 50 < 20 < price) = strong uptrend

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
- **Signal line crossover** — MACD line crosses above signal line = bullish. Below = bearish
- **Zero line cross** — MACD crosses above zero = momentum shift to bullish
- **Histogram** — bars growing = momentum building. Shrinking = momentum fading
- **Divergence** — price makes new high but MACD doesn't = bearish divergence (warning). Vice versa = bullish

## Momentum Indicators

### RSI (Relative Strength Index) — 14 period default
- **>70** = Overbought (potential reversal or pause). In strong uptrends, can stay overbought long
- **<30** = Oversold (potential bounce). In strong downtrends, can stay oversold long
- **50 line** = Bullish if RSI holds above 50. Bearish if fails to reclaim 50
- **Divergence** = Strongest RSI signal. Price new high + RSI lower high = sell. Price new low + RSI higher low = buy
- **Best use** = Confirmation, not standalone. Combine with trend and volume

### Stochastic RSI
- More sensitive version. Good for short-term timing
- Cross above 20 = buy signal. Cross below 80 = sell signal

### Bollinger Bands
- **Band squeeze** = Volatility contraction → breakout coming (direction unknown until it breaks)
- **Band walk** = Price hugging upper band = strong uptrend. Lower band = strong downtrend
- **Mean reversion** = Price touches outer band, look for reversal back to middle

## Volume Analysis

Volume is the fuel. Price moves on low volume are suspect. High volume confirms moves.

- **Volume spike on breakout** = confirms the move, likely to continue
- **Volume dry up on pullback** = normal, healthy. Buyers not selling into weakness
- **Volume spike on breakdown** = distribution, smart money exiting
- **Rising price, falling volume** = warning sign. Move may lack conviction
- **OBV (On Balance Volume)** = If OBV trending up while price flat = accumulation. Good buy signal

**RVOL (Relative Volume)** = Today's volume / 10-day avg volume
- RVOL > 1.5 = above average interest, breakouts more reliable
- RVOL > 3.0 = news or event driven, high risk/reward

## Support & Resistance

### How to identify
- Previous highs and lows (swing highs/lows)
- Round numbers ($100, $50, ₹500 etc.)
- High-volume price levels (seen on volume profile)
- Moving averages (dynamic S/R)
- Fibonacci retracement levels (0.382, 0.5, 0.618)

### Key rule
Once support is broken, it becomes resistance. Once resistance is broken, it becomes support. Called a "role reversal."

### Fibonacci Levels (in pullback context)
- 0.382 = shallow pullback in strong uptrend — buy zone
- 0.5 = moderate pullback — normal
- 0.618 = deep pullback — last line before trend break. Strong buy if holds
- 0.786 = very deep. Trend likely weakening

## Chart Patterns

### Bullish Patterns
- **Cup and Handle** — consolidation forms a cup shape, handle is small pullback. Breakout above handle = buy
- **Bull Flag** — sharp move up, then tight sideways/slight down consolidation. Breakout = continuation
- **Ascending Triangle** — flat resistance, rising lows. Bullish resolution expected
- **Inverse Head and Shoulders** — reversal pattern. Three lows (middle lowest). Break of neckline = buy

### Bearish Patterns
- **Head and Shoulders** — three peaks (middle highest). Neckline break = sell
- **Bear Flag** — sharp move down, then tight bounce. Breakdown continues
- **Descending Triangle** — falling highs, flat support. Bearish resolution expected
- **Double Top** — two peaks at same resistance. Break below valley between = sell

### Consolidation/Neutral
- **Symmetrical Triangle** — converging highs and lows. Breakout in direction of trend
- **Rectangle/Range** — price bouncing between flat S/R. Trade the range or the breakout

## Candlestick Signals (key ones)

### Reversal Signals
- **Hammer** (after downtrend) — small body, long lower wick. Buyers rejected the lows
- **Shooting Star** (after uptrend) — small body, long upper wick. Sellers rejected the highs
- **Doji** — open ≈ close. Indecision. Matters when it appears at key levels after a trend
- **Engulfing candle** — large candle completely engulfs prior candle. Bullish engulf = buy, bearish = sell
- **Morning Star / Evening Star** — 3-candle reversal pattern. Strong signal

### Continuation Signals
- **Marubozu** — no wicks. Strong directional conviction
- **Inside bar** — small candle within prior candle's range. Coil before breakout

## Reading a Chart — Order of Operations

1. Start with higher timeframe (weekly) to identify primary trend
2. Drop to daily for setup context
3. Use hourly/15min for precise entry timing
4. Check volume confirms the move
5. Check 1-2 momentum indicators (RSI + MACD)
6. Identify the key support and resistance levels
7. Define entry, stop, and target BEFORE entering
