# Market Psychology & Sentiment Reference

## Sentiment Indicators

### VIX (Volatility Index / "Fear Index")
- Measures implied volatility of S&P 500 options = market's expectation of near-term volatility
- **<15** = Complacency. Market calm, often near tops or sustained bull run
- **15–25** = Normal range
- **25–35** = Elevated fear. Pullback or correction in progress
- **>35** = Extreme fear. Capitulation zone. Historically a buying opportunity
- **>50** = Crisis level (COVID March 2020, GFC 2008–09)

**Contrarian use:** Extreme fear (VIX >35) = market likely oversold. Extreme greed (VIX <12) = watch for complacency

### Fear & Greed Index (CNN)
Scale 0–100: 0 = Extreme Fear, 100 = Extreme Greed
- **0–25** = Extreme Fear → historically good buying zone
- **25–45** = Fear → cautious, look for quality
- **45–55** = Neutral
- **55–75** = Greed → be selective
- **75–100** = Extreme Greed → reduce exposure, be careful

Composed of: stock price momentum, stock price strength, market breadth, put/call ratio, junk bond demand, market volatility, safe haven demand.

### Put/Call Ratio
- Total put options traded ÷ Total call options traded
- **>1.0** = More puts than calls = bearish sentiment (potential contrarian buy)
- **<0.7** = More calls = bullish sentiment (potential contrarian sell)
- Best used as a contrarian indicator at extremes

### AAII Sentiment Survey (Weekly)
- American Association of Individual Investors — weekly survey of bullish/bearish %
- When **bearish % >50%** = historically very bullish (contrarian)
- When **bullish % >55%** = market often due for pullback
- Follow the smart money, fade the crowd at extremes

## Market Breadth Indicators

### Advance/Decline Line (A/D Line)
- Number of advancing stocks minus declining stocks, cumulative
- **A/D Line rising with index** = healthy, broad participation
- **A/D Line falling while index rises** = divergence, rally being led by fewer stocks (warning)
- Narrow leadership = fragile rally

### % of Stocks Above 200 MA
- **>70%** = Strong bull market
- **50–70%** = Healthy
- **30–50%** = Mixed, selective
- **<30%** = Bear market conditions
- A rally with only 40% of stocks above 200MA is a "zombie rally"

### New 52-Week Highs vs Lows
- More new highs than lows = healthy market
- More lows than highs while index is up = deteriorating breadth (divergence = warning)

## Institutional Behavior

### Follow the Smart Money

**Institutional buying signals:**
- Large block trades (>10,000 shares) at market price or above ask
- Unusual options activity (large call buying, especially out-of-the-money)
- Stock consolidating quietly with rising OBV (On-Balance Volume)
- 13F filings show major fund increasing stake

**Institutional selling signals:**
- High volume down days ("distribution days")
- Stock can't hold gains after good news
- Rising price on declining OBV
- Multiple distribution days in 4–5 week period

### Insider Transactions (SEC Form 4)
- **Insider buying = strong signal** (they're spending their own money, only one reason to buy)
- **Insider selling = weak signal** (many reasons: diversification, lifestyle, RSU vesting — not necessarily bearish)
- Most bullish: CEO/CFO open-market buying in size, multiple insiders buying simultaneously
- Most bearish: CEO selling right after an earnings beat, sudden mass insider selling

### Short Interest
- Short float % = shares sold short ÷ float
- **>20% short float** = heavily shorted. High risk, but also high potential for short squeeze
- **Short squeeze** = when heavily shorted stock rises, forcing shorts to cover, accelerating the move
- Days to cover = short shares ÷ avg daily volume. >5 days = squeeze potential if positive catalyst hits

## Market Cycles

### Economic Cycle → Sector Rotation

| Cycle Phase | Characteristics | Best Sectors |
|---|---|---|
| Early Recovery | Rates low, economy recovering from recession | Financials, Consumer Discretionary, Tech, Industrials |
| Mid Expansion | GDP growing, confidence high | Tech, Industrials, Materials, Real Estate |
| Late Expansion | Inflation rising, rates rising | Energy, Materials, Healthcare, Consumer Staples |
| Recession/Contraction | GDP falling, unemployment rising | Consumer Staples, Healthcare, Utilities, Cash |

### Bull/Bear Market Psychology Stages

**Bull Market:**
1. Disbelief — "This rally can't last, I'll wait for the dip"
2. Skepticism — "Okay it's held, but I'm not convinced"
3. Acceptance — "Okay it's a real bull market, I'm buying"
4. Euphoria — "Stocks only go up, put everything in" ← TOP IS HERE

**Bear Market:**
1. Denial — "Just a healthy correction, buy the dip"
2. Fear — "This is bad, should I sell?"
3. Capitulation — "I can't take it anymore, selling everything" ← BOTTOM IS HERE
4. Despair — "Markets are broken, I'll never invest again"

**The best entry points** = Disbelief/Skepticism (bull market) and Capitulation/Despair (bear market)  
**The worst entry points** = Euphoria (top) and Denial (early bear, more pain coming)

## Behavioral Patterns to Exploit in Others

### Earnings Overreaction
- Stock beats estimates but drops ("sell the news")
- Or misses but rallies ("worst already priced in")
- Best approach: wait 2–3 days post-earnings for dust to settle. Knee-jerk moves often reverse.

### Gap Fill Theory
- Most gaps on daily charts eventually "fill" (price returns to where it gapped from)
- Useful for: identifying downside target if stock gapped up, or upside target if gapped down
- Not a rule, but a tendency worth knowing

### Seasonal Patterns
- **January Effect** — small caps historically outperform in January (tax-loss selling reversal)
- **"Sell in May and Go Away"** — May–October historically weaker than Nov–April
- **Santa Claus Rally** — last 5 trading days of year + first 2 of new year often bullish
- **Pre-earnings drift** — stocks with strong beat history tend to drift up in the 2 weeks before earnings
- These are tendencies, not laws. Don't trade on them alone.

### Mean Reversion vs Momentum
- Stocks in the **middle of a range** → trade mean reversion (fade extremes)
- Stocks at **52-week highs with volume** → trade momentum (go with the trend)
- The mistake: applying mean reversion to trending stocks (shorting new highs) or applying momentum to range-bound stocks

## Sentiment-Based Signals (Contrarian)

| Situation | What Crowd Does | What to Consider |
|---|---|---|
| Market crashes 20%+ | Panic sell, go to cash | Start buying quality in tranches |
| Stock up 50%+ in a month | FOMO buying at top | Look for exit, not entry |
| Every retail investor is bullish on X | Buy more X | Actually a warning sign |
| "This time is different" | Rationalize crazy valuations | It almost never is |
| Business media says "stocks are doomed" | Sell everything | Often marks bottoms |
| IPO frenzy, SPACs everywhere, crypto on CNBC | Everyone is a genius | Near market top |
